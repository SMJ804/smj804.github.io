  2. A body falls in the gravitational field of the earth. Show that its acceleration is independent of its mass

    Answer: Gravitational force $$ F = \frac{GMm}{r^2} $$ We know \( F = ma \) so $$ ma = \frac{GMm}{r^2}$$ or $$ a = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$ thus we can see that acceration due to gravity does not depend on the mass of the falling body.
